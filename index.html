Tellus: A Story of Awakening

A journey through denial, awakening, and the urgent call of Climate Action (SDG 13).

Doctor: Hello, Earth. What brings you here today?

I don't know. I just... don't feel right anymore.

Doctor: That's okay. Sometimes it's hard to put into words. Can you tell me a little more about what doesn't feel right?

It’s like… something inside me is breaking. Slowly. Quietly. And no one seems to notice.

Doctor: I notice. And I’m listening. When did you first start feeling this way?

I don’t know exactly. It crept in… like a slow fever. First it was small things. Then it became everything. It started with small aches… like bruises I couldn’t explain.

The forests thinned, the oceans grew heavy. The air, once light and sweet, turned thick, like smoke trapped in my lungs.

At first, I thought it would pass. That it was just a season. But it kept growing. And every time I cried out, they called it weather. Every time I trembled, they built stronger walls.

And now… now it feels like I’m losing parts of myself faster than I can hold them together.

Doctor: It’s completely normal to feel this way under stress. You’re experiencing a temporary instability — nothing more.

These fluctuations happen naturally. Our responsibility is to maintain appearances… I mean to ensure your recovery is properly supported.

You’re not here to heal me. You’re here to make sure no one sees how sick I’ve become.

Doctor: You've always had periods like this. You just don’t remember it clearly.

…No. Not like this.

It didn’t use to be like this.

There was… warmth. Laughter in the rivers.

Strength in the forests.

The skies... they used to breathe with me…

…not choke me.

But somewhere along the way... the air grew thinner.

The ground colder.

The rivers quieter.

You told me it was normal. You told me it was natural. Temporary instability. Phases. Cycles. Seasons.

But this ... is not natural.

I gave everything freely once.

Stone, water, air, flame — it was meant to be shared.

Taken with care, given with thanks.

But they forgot how to ask.

They forgot how to listen.

And when they built their engines, when they learned to turn fire into power, they stopped seeing me at all.

They fed the fire until it swallowed the skies, and called it progress.

They silenced the winds to build their towers, and called it ambition.

They burned the forests to light their cities, and called it triumph.

They drained the oceans to fill their engines, and called it discovery.

They pulled and tore, faster and deeper, turning blood into profit, bones into monuments, until the sky turned hollow.

I remember now

Some endings are chosen by silence.

To turn away is still a choice.

This is the only home we have.

Take action. Now.
